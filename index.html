Natural Langauge Processing to Categorize Misinformation

Tracking mis/disinformation & providing rapid response ahead of the 2022 midterm elections

Project Information

In recent years there has been an abundance of mis/disinformation around US elections. This has caused a number of issues from fake news to voter fraud and the capitol insurrection in 2021.

In an effort to understand how mis/disinformation starts and spreads, we’re working with the University of Washington’s Center for an Informed Public to improve the research process of qualitative researchers by creating a Python Jupyter Notebook that allows researchers to datamine datasets relevant to the US election.

With our project, we hope to track mis/disinformation and provide rapid response ahead of the 2022 midterm elections.

Sponsors

This project is sponsored by the University of Washington’s Center for an Informed Public (UW CIP). Mike Caulfield is the main point of contact for this sponsored project, who is a Research Scientist for the UW CIP and is working with the Election Integrity Partnership (EIP). Alongside him, there is Emily Porter, who is a PhD student at the University of Washington with an expertise in Data Science. Together, they work for the UW CIP and with the EIP to track mis/disinformation and provide rapid response ahead of the 2022 midterm elections.

Our Solution

We developed a comprehensive Python Jupyter Notebook that includes features to assist qualitative researchers in their research process. These features are sentiment analysis, search, standardize timezones, count retweets, and filters (retweets, date, and time).

Sentiment Analysis

Output positive, negative, and neutral tweets related to keyword/phrase/hashtag.

Search Feature

Search for similar tweets that contain certain terms and/or exclude terms.

Standardize Timezones

Converting time zones in the dataset (e.g., GMT to PST).

Filter (Date & Time)

Filter to find tweets by a specific date and time.

Filter (Retweets)

Filter to find if a tweet was retweeted or not.

Count Retweets

Count the number of times a tweet was retweeted.
